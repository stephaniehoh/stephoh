what ive done

Sharing music is more than just sending names and links. It's about enjoying the same experience - at the same time, near or far. Every listener in your private room can also play, pause, skip, and choose whichever song comes next. It can be a group of friends studying together at the library, or a couple in a long distance relationship going on a run at the same time.

Built in 48 hours at the Launch Hackathon, we were very excited about this app because we decided to implement websockets and channels on our own instead of using real-time wrappers like Firebase or Pusher.

Site: mylittledisco.herokuapp.com
Code: www.github.com/jonjchew/mylittledisco

We wanted to take shopping back to its traditional roots as a simple visual experience. See more similar products as you scroll based on what you 'like'. More information will only be revealed if you're interested, just as if you were inspecting the tag at the store.

Deliberate simple UI with a complex modular backend. This was built during my first two weeks of exposure to Ruby on Rails. We implemented background workers and database caching to maneuver around API querying limits.

Site: shop-me.herokuapp.com
Code: www.github.com/jonjchew/shop-me
